Using Bimanual Haptics in Virtual Assembly Tasks

I've worked with Dr. Judy Vance and other graduate students on research using various configurations of bimanual haptic devices to examine their effectiveness in virtual assembly tasks. One of the haptic devices we use is the Geomagic Touch (formerly Sensable Phantom Omni). This research aims to improve the training of assembly tasks through the utilization of haptic devices, virtual reality, and accurate physics calculations. We've also examined how much learning transfer takes place in using this technology for virtual training.
